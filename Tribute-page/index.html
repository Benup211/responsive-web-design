Tara Devi

Nightingale of Nepal

Tara Devi's Life Timeline

  • 15 January 1946: Tara Devi (Dol Kumari Karki) is born in the neighborhood of Indra Chowk in Kathmandu, Nepal, to Krishna Bahadur Karki and Radha Devi Karki.
  • 1951: At the age of 5, Tara Devi goes to Radio Nepal and gets the opportunity to sing, impressing the crowd with her soulful voice.
  • Early 1960s: She starts her professional singing career at the age of seven, primarily singing for children's programs at Radio Nepal.
  • 1966: Tara Devi marries Shiva Bahadur Shrestha, an aircraft pilot.
  • Throughout her career: Tara Devi records over 4,000 songs, becoming a prominent figure in Nepalese music.
  • Throughout her career: She records a wide range of songs, including prayer songs, Nepali folk songs, and classic numbers.
  • Throughout her career: She earns various awards and honors for her contributions to music, including the Member of the Order of Gorkha Dakshina Bahu, First Class, Mahendra-Ratna Award, Indra Rajya Laxmi Award, Jagadamba Shree Puraskar, and the Chinnalata award.
  • 1970s: Tara Devi's singing career progresses, and she rises from earning Rs. 5 per song at Radio Nepal to Rs. 100 per song.
  • 30-year career at Radio Nepal: Tara Devi is appointed as a Kharidar at Radio Nepal and is later promoted to the position of Secretary.
  • 1980s: She becomes famously known as the "Nightingale of Nepal."
  • Later in life: Tara Devi endures difficult periods, including the loss of her 25-year-old son to blood cancer.
  • Later in life: Her husband, Shiva Bahadur Shrestha, dies in an airplane crash.
  • Late in life: Tara Devi's health starts deteriorating, and she is diagnosed with Parkinson's disease, which leads to a halt in her singing career.
  • 21 January 2006: Tara Devi passes away at the age of 60.
  • Her last album, "Afanta Ko Manma": Contains her last four songs.
  • Survived by: Tara Devi is survived by a son and a daughter.
